# License Change

Starting with the commit that adds this notice, and with the next release,
`nah` is licensed under the Functional Source License 1.1 with MIT as the
Future License (`FSL-1.1-MIT`).

Versions and source snapshots made available under MIT before this change remain
MIT. The last tagged MIT release is `v0.7.1`, and versions up to and including
`v0.7.1` remain licensed under MIT.

Each `FSL-1.1-MIT` release converts to MIT two years after the date that release
is made available.

In plain English: you can use `nah` for personal projects, open-source work,
research, evaluation, and internal company workflows. Companies can use `nah`
inside their own engineering workflows.

Recent versions may not be repackaged, hosted, white-labeled, resold, or offered
as a competing coding-agent, developer-tooling, security, governance, or
permissions product.

This change is meant to protect `nah` from vendor capture while keeping it
useful and accessible to developers. `nah` remains source-available and
fair-source.

The short version: internal use is fine; turning recent `nah` into a competing
product or hosted service is not.

For uses outside the license terms, contact the maintainer through the
repository.
